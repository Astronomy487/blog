$0^0$

This is kinda a viral problem on social media. What number is $0^0$ equal to? The typical answers are:

  • $0^0$ is $1$ because $\forall x:x^0=1$
  • $0^0$ is $0$ because $\forall x:0^x=0$
  • $0^0$ is undefined; I am either copping out of committing to this problem, or I recognize $0^0$ as an indeterminate form from calculus that doesn't meet the conditions to be turned into a less indeterminate form

There's a lot of ways you can approach this. One might be to consider the limits of the functions $\cc{0^x}$, $\cm{x^0}$, and $\cy{x^x}$ as $x$ approaches $0$.

$\cc{0^x}$ approaches $0$, but only from the right.

$\cm{x^0}$ approaches $1$, from both sides.

$\cy{x^x}$ approaches $1$, but only from the right. You could maybe point out some convergence towards $1$ from the left if you're willing to see this situation as complex.

I like the copout answer of $0^0$ is undefined.

This is because I'm really suspicious of $0^0$: when does it actually crop up? Pretty much only in posts feeding on the conflict between the two possible answers.

There are sometimes contexts where I'll want to talk about the functions $\cc{0^x}$ or $\cm{x^0}$ evaluated at $x=0$, in which case choosing their respective limits makes sense. But it feels like $0^0$ on its own is only ever written to confuse people.
